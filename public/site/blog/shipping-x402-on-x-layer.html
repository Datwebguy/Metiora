Shipping x402 on X Layer without the usual rejection traps

What actually fails marketplace review, and how Metiora hardens against it.

When OKX reviews an ASP, they do not grade your landing page first. They hit your endpoint unpaid, decode the payment requirements, check the fee token, then try to complete a paid path. Most rejections we studied came from small mismatches, not missing ambition.

Match the fee token exactly

X Layer mainnet USDT0 is 0x779ded0c9e1022225f8e0630b35a9b54be713736. If the listing says that address but the live 402 response quotes another chain USDT, validation can look fine while real pay fails. Metiora forces network eip155:196 and publishes the same token in catalog and unpaid bodies.

Use permanent public HTTPS URLs

Localhost, private IPs, and temporary tunnels fail review. Metiora serves product paths on https://agentmetiora.xyz with TLS at the edge. The resource URL in x402 must be that same string buyers will call.

Return 402, not hang

Unpaid POSTs return payment required quickly. Paid generation has a hard timeout so clients get JSON instead of a dead connection. Machines stay warm so cold starts do not look like outages during review.

Publish required body fields

After pay, Metiora expects founder and startup profile IDs. Those fields appear in the services catalog and 402 body so buyers are not surprised with a silent 400 after settlement logic starts.
